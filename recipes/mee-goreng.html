Mee Goreng

Description

Mee goreng is a popular Malaysian fried noodle dish typically served with fried bean curd, boiled potatoes and squid. It is commonly prepared and sold by the Indian Muslim community in Malaysia. The dish packs a delightful combination of flavors: sweet, spicy, tangy and savory. Therefore, none of your taste buds is spared when you are savoring this dish!

Ingredients

Yield: 4 people

Fried noodles

  • 2 1/2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • 1 potato boiled, peeled and sliced
  • 3 pieces fried bean curd cut into pieces
  • 4 squid cut into rings
  • 1 lb yellow noodles
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup bean sprouts
  • 1 lime cut into wedges
  • lettuce leaves sliced for garnishing

Chili paste

  • 10 dried red chillies
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 tablespoon water

Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons dark soy sauce or kicap manis
  • 3 tablespoons ketchup
  • sugar to taste

Steps

  1. Blend all chili paste ingredients in a food blender.
  2. Heat the wok and fry the chili paste blended mix until the oil separates out. Set aside.
  3. Mix the sauce ingredients together in a small bowl. Set aside.
  4. Fire up the wok on high heat and add 2 tablespoons of cooking oil. Add the garlic, 3 tablespoons of chili paste, potatoes, bean curd and squid. Fry until fragrant. Add the noodles and the sauce and keep stirring. Push the noodles to the side of the wok.
  5. Add the remaining 1/2 tablespoon of cooking oil and crack the eggs over the oil. Scramble the eggs and mix in with the noodles. Add the bean sprouts and stir for about 1 minute.
  6. Dish out and garnish with sliced lettuce leaves and lime wedges. Squeeze a bit of lime juice over the noodles before serving.
